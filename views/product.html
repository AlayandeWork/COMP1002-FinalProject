About this item:

The Nintendo 64, often abbreviated as N64, is a video game console developed and manufactured by Nintendo. It was released in 1996 in Japan and North America and in 1997 in Europe and Australia. The N64 was the successor to the Super Nintendo Entertainment System (SNES) and competed with other consoles of its generation, primarily the Sony PlayStation and the Sega Saturn.

The Nintendo 64 (N64) is a video game console released by Nintendo in 1996. It featured 64-bit architecture, cartridge-based games, and the iconic three-pronged controller with an analog stick. Known for its strong lineup of first-party titles like Super Mario 64 and The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, as well as popular multiplayer experiences, the N64 left a lasting impact on gaming culture despite being discontinued in 2002.
